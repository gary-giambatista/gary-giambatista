Software Engineer
-----------------

It took a long time for me to find my passion in building software and applications. I started like many, a business student who went on to work in real estate and later at an advertising agency doing marketing. With a clear eye on what my future was, I decided to walk a different path, one which took me overseas to Japan for over 3 years. Living abroad was the best experience of my life. I worked as an English teacher and got to teach many children while learning about a new culture and way of life. While life was amazing, I sought a path to bring more value into the world. That path led me to fostering many ideas to create things of value, and software has become my tool of choice for making my ideas into real life utilties. 

While developing my first app, Esteem, and being deeply inspired by web3 I dropped everything and left my life behind in Japan to become a full-time developer. Some of the first lines of code I wrote were in Solidity when I launched my ERC-1155 NFT on OpenSea 1 year ago. Given my background, I don't consider myself to be the "standard engineering type", but I think that makes me a unique asset.

*“Being a servant to the user is your job when you are a developer, you want to be producing something that other people are going to find valuable. And, if you are technically inclined, then finding the right levers to be able to pull to be able to make a design that’s going to produce the most value for the least amount effort.” How to be a great developer by,* **John Carmack**
